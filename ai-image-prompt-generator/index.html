About This Tool

This AI image prompt generator helps you build detailed, structured prompts for text-to-image AI tools like Midjourney, DALL-E 3, and Stable Diffusion. Rather than guessing which words to use, you select from proven descriptors for style, lighting, mood, and composition — then the tool assembles them into a format that consistently produces better images.

Why prompts matter for AI image generation

AI image generators are powerful, but they're sensitive to how you describe things. A vague prompt like "a forest at night" can produce anything from a dark horror scene to a calm moonlit woodland. Adding specific lighting ("moonlight filtering through trees"), mood ("peaceful and serene"), and style ("oil painting") dramatically narrows what the AI produces — and gets you much closer to what you actually imagined.

Professional AI artists spend as much time on their prompts as beginners do re-generating images dozens of times. Learning which descriptors to use — and in what order — is one of the key skills of working effectively with image generation tools.

Prompt order matters in Midjourney: Words at the beginning of your prompt carry more weight than words at the end. Put your most important descriptors (subject, style) first, and quality modifiers like "highly detailed" or "8K" at the end.

Differences between platforms

  • Midjourney responds well to artistic style descriptors, artist name references, and the --ar ratio parameter. It tends to produce more aesthetically polished results by default.
  • DALL-E 3 (via ChatGPT) understands natural language descriptions better and handles complex scenes well. It's more literal than Midjourney — describe exactly what you want rather than using shorthand terms.
  • Stable Diffusion benefits from explicit technical descriptors and negative prompts (telling the model what NOT to include). It's the most customizable but requires more prompt engineering.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use these prompts directly in Midjourney?

Yes. Copy the generated prompt and paste it directly into the Midjourney Discord bot using the /imagine command. The --ar parameter for aspect ratio is included automatically when you select one.

How many style tags should I select?

Two or three style descriptors is usually ideal. Selecting too many conflicting styles (e.g. "oil painting" and "pixel art" together) can confuse the model and produce inconsistent results. Pick the style that matters most and add one complementary descriptor.

Is my prompt saved anywhere?

No. This tool runs entirely in your browser. Nothing is uploaded or stored. Your prompts stay on your device.
